The "Day 1" Experience: A structured onboarding reduces turnover by 30%. Supervisor must guide the new hire through these steps on their first day.

Phase 1: Logistics & Access

Action Item Initials
Badge & Timeclock: Issued ID badge and demonstrated how to clock in/out.
Parking & Entry: Showed designated parking and employee entrance.
Lockers/Personal Items: Assigned locker. Reviewed "No Cell Phone" zones.
Restrooms & Breakroom: Showed location of facilities and refrigerator policy.

Phase 2: Introduction to Safety

Action Item Initials
PPE Issue: Employee has received Safety Glasses, Boots, and Vest.
Emergency Stops: Walked the line and pointed out E-Stops.
Rally Point: Physically walked to the Fire Evacuation Rally Point.
Eye Wash / Shower: Showed location of nearest eye wash station.
SDS Station: Showed location of "Right to Know" station.

Phase 3: Supervisor Observation

Supervisor must observe the employee performing actual work to verify competence.

A. Body Mechanics

Behavior Safe Coach
Lifting with legs, not back?
Keeping loads close to the body?
Avoiding twisting while carrying?

B. Tool & Equipment Use

Behavior Safe Coach
Inspects tools before use?
Uses correct tool for the job (no improvisation)?
Wears PPE consistently without reminders?
Decision Point: By the end of Week 1, is this employee safe enough to work independently?
✅ RELEASE TO WORK
Employee has demonstrated safe work habits and is approved for solo work.
❌ EXTEND TRAINING
Employee needs more supervision. Do not release.
