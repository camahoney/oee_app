# New Hire Safety Orientation: "Day One" Defense
**Version:** 1.0
**Format:** PowerPoint (12 Slides)
**Purpose:** To establish a baseline of safety awareness for EVERY new employee before they touch a tool.

---

## Slide 1: Welcome to the Team
**Visual:** Photo of the team working safely, smiling. Branded overlay.
**Text:** New Employee Safety Orientation. "Production is important. Safety is non-negotiable."
**Speaker Notes:** "Welcome. You were hired because you're good at what you do. But we also hired you to retire from here with all your fingers and toes. This orientation covers the absolute basics you need to survive Day 1."

## Slide 2: The Logic (Why We Do This)
**Visual:** Simple chart showing "Accidents happen most in Month 1".
**Text:**
*   **Fact:** 40% of workplace injuries happen to employees with less than 6 months tenure.
*   **Goal:** We want you to beat the odds.
*   **Your Job:** Ask questions. If you don't know, DON'T GUESS.
**Speaker Notes:** "You are at the highest risk right now because you don't know what you don't know. We expect you to stop and ask."

## Slide 3: Emergency Action Plan (EAP)
**Visual:** Floor plan diagram with "You Are Here" and "Exit" icons.
**Text:**
*   **Fire:** Leave via nearest exit. Gather at [Rally Point].
*   **Tornado/Weather:** Move to the interior shelter [Restrooms/Hallway].
*   **Medical:** yell "Medic!" or call ext [1234]. Do not move the victim unless in immediate danger.
**Speaker Notes:** "Memorize your exits. If the alarm sounds, drop your tools and go. Do not go to your locker, do not go to your car."

## Slide 4: PPE (Personal Protective Equipment)
**Visual:** Icon grid of Hard Hat, Glasses, Vest, Boots, Gloves.
**Text:**
*   **Condition of Employment:** No PPE = No Work.
*   **Standard Kit:** Safety Glasses (Z87), Steel/Composite Toe Boots, High-Vis Vest.
*   **Care:** Inspect it daily. If it's broken, trade it in for new gear immediately.
**Speaker Notes:** "This gear is your last line of defense. We provide it for free. You just have to wear it. If your glasses are scratched, get new ones from the cabinet."

## Slide 5: Hazard Communication (Chemicals)
**Visual:** GHS Pictogram Chart (Diamond shapes with red borders).
**Text:**
*   **Right to Know:** You have the right to know what chemicals you work with.
*   **SDS (Safety Data Sheets):** Located in the yellow binder in the breakroom (or online).
*   **Labeling:** Never put chemicals in unmarked water bottles.
**Speaker Notes:** "If you see a bottle with blue liquid and no label, don't touch it. Tell your supervisor. Read the label before you use anything."

## Slide 6: Lockout / Tagout (Simple Awareness)
**Visual:** A Red Lock on a machine switch.
**Text:**
*   **What it means:** The machine is de-energized for repair.
*   **Your Rule:** NEVER remove a lock that isn't yours.
*   **Result:** Removing a lock can kill the mechanic working inside. Immediate termination.
**Speaker Notes:** "This red lock saves lives. If you see one, the machine is off limits. Period."

## Slide 7: Bloodborne Pathogens
**Visual:** "Biohazard" symbol.
**Text:**
*   **Rule:** Do not touch someone else's blood or bodily fluids.
*   **Action:** If someone is bleeding, call the First Responders.
*   **Cleanup:** Only trained personnel can clean up blood spills.
**Speaker Notes:** "We have a specific team trained to handle injuries. Your job is to call for help and comfort the injured person—but protect yourself first."

## Slide 8: Slips, Trips, and Falls
**Visual:** Photo of a cord stretched across a walkway (staged).
**Text:**
*   **#1 Cause of Injury:** Gravity.
*   **Housekeeping:** Clean as you go. Don't leave cords in aisles.
*   **3 Points of Contact:** Use handrails on all stairs.
**Speaker Notes:** "A clean shop is a safe shop. Most injuries here aren't from explosions—they're from tripping over a pallet jack. Watch your step."

## Slide 9: Equipment Safety (Guarding)
**Visual:** Machine guard highlighted in yellow.
**Text:**
*   **Guards:** Keep fingers out of pinch points.
*   **Tampering:** Never bypass a guard or limit switch.
*   **Authorized Use:** Do not operate a forklift or crane unless you have a card in your wallet saying you can.
**Speaker Notes:** "Just because you drove a forklift at your last job doesn't mean you can drive ours. You must be certified here first."

## Slide 10: Reporting Accidents & Near Misses
**Visual:** Triangle Safety Pyramid.
**Text:**
*   **Report Everything:** Even a paper cut. Even a "close call".
*   **Why?** Reporting a "Near Miss" lets us fix the hazard before someone actually gets hurt.
*   **Timing:** Report immediately—not at the end of the shift.
**Speaker Notes:** "We don't punish people for reporting injuries. We get mad when you hide them. If you get hurt, tell us now so we can treat it."

## Slide 11: Stop Work Authority
**Visual:** Large Open Hand / Stop Sign.
**Text:**
*   **Your Right:** You have the authority to stop any job you think is unsafe.
*   **No Retaliation:** You will be thanked, not fired.
*   **Gut Check:** If it feels wrong, it probably is. Stop and ask.
**Speaker Notes:** "This is the most important slide. You are the expert on your own safety. If a manager tells you to do something unsafe, you say STOP."

## Slide 12: The Pledge
**Visual:** Text of the safety pledge.
**Text:**
*   I agree to follow these rules.
*   I will wear my PPE.
*   I will report all hazards.
*   I want to go home safe.
*   **[Sign the Training Roster]**
**Speaker Notes:** "Welcome to the team. Let's get to work."
