Program Scope
Section: 1.0 General

1.1 Purpose

This program establishes the minimum requirements for the control of hazardous energy. It ensures that machines are isolated from all potentially hazardous energy, and locked out or tagged out, before employees perform any servicing or maintenance activities where the unexpected energization, start-up, or release of stored energy could cause injury.

1.2 Scope & Application

This program applies to the control of energy during servicing and/or maintenance of machines and equipment. Normal production operations are not covered by this standard unless:

  • An employee is required to remove or bypass a guard or other safety device.
  • An employee is required to place any part of their body into an area on a machine or piece of equipment where work is actually performed upon the material being processed (point of operation).
⚠
Zero Tolerance Policy: Any employee found violating LOTO procedures, including removing another person's lock or bypassing a lock, constitutes a Class A safety violation and is subject to immediate termination.

Core Procedures
Section: 2.0 Execution

1.3 Definitions

Authorized Employee

A person who locks out or tags out machines or equipment in order to perform servicing or maintenance on that machine or equipment.

Affected Employee

An employee whose job requires them to operate or use a machine or equipment on which servicing or maintenance is being performed under lockout or tagout.

2.1 The 6-Step LOTO Procedure

All authorized employees must follow these six steps for every lockout event:

Step 1: Preparation

Notify affected employees. Identify all energy sources (Electrical, Pneumatic, Hydraulic, Thermal, Gravity, Chemical).

Step 2: Shutdown

Shut down the machine using normal operating controls (Stop Button, Disconnect switch).

Step 3: Isolation

Physically isolate the machine from energy sources. Open breakers, close valves, disconnect lines.

Step 4: Application

Apply LOTO devices. Requirements: Lock must be substantial, standardized (Red for LOTO), and identifiable (Name tag).

Step 5: Dissipation

Relieve stored energy. Bleed air lines, drain capacitors, block gravity-fed rams.

Step 6: Verification (Tryout)

CRITICAL: Attempt to start the machine using operator controls. Verify it does not energize. Return controls to "Neutral/Off".

2.2 Removal of Locks (Startup)

  1. Inspect machine area to ensure non-essential items (tools) have been removed.
  2. Check that all employees are safely positioned away from the machine.
  3. Verify controls are in neutral.
  4. Remove LOTO devices.
  5. Notify affected employees that the machine is ready for use.

Special Situations
Section: 3.0 Advanced

3.1 Group Lockout

When multiple authorized employees are servicing the same machine:

  • A Primary Authorized Employee applies a single lock to the machine.
  • The key to that lock is placed in a Group Lockbox.
  • Each authorized employee applies their OWN personal lock to the Group Lockbox.
  • Result: The key cannot be retrieved (and the machine cannot start) until the LAST employee removes their lock.

3.2 Shift Change

To ensure continuity of protection between shifts:

  • The incoming employee applies their lock before the outgoing employee removes theirs.
  • If a face-to-face handoff is not possible, the outgoing employee leaves their lock on. The incoming employee applies their lock, then the supervisor cuts the old lock (following Section 3.3).

3.3 Emergency Lock Removal (Abandoned Lock)

If an employee leaves the facility with their lock still attached, it may only be removed by the Site Manager under these strict conditions:

  1. Verify the employee is physically NOT on the premises.
  2. Make all reasonable efforts to contact the employee (phone call).
  3. Ensure the employee is informed before they resume work that their lock was removed.

4.1 Periodic Inspection (Annual Audit)

An authorized employee (other than the one(s) utilizing the energy control procedure) must inspect the procedure at least annually.

  • Purpose: To correct any deviations or inadequacies.
  • Scope: Review of the written procedure + observation of the employee applying it.
  • Documentation: Needs to identify the machine, the date, employees included, and inspector.

4.2 Training Requirements

Authorized Employees

Training on recognition of hazardous energy, type and magnitude of energy in the facility, and methods for isolation and control.

Affected Employees

Instruction on the purpose and use of the energy control procedure and the prohibition against attempting to restart locked machines.

4.3 Retraining

Retraining shall be provided whenever there is a change in job assignments, machines, equipment, or processes that present a new hazard, or when the periodic inspection reveals inadequacies in the employee's knowledge.

Section: 5.0 External

5.1 Outside Personnel (Contractors)

Whenever outside servicing personnel are to be engaged in activities covered by the scope and application of this standard, the on-site employer and the outside employer shall inform each other of their respective lockout or tagout procedures.

Rule of Thumb: Vetted Safe recommends that contractors use their own locks (distinct color preferred) but must follow the facility's specific energy isolation procedures.

5.2 Tagout Systems (Limitations)

Tags are essentially warning devices affixed to energy isolating devices and do not provide the physical restraint on those devices that is provided by a lock.

  • Tags must be legible and understandable.
  • Tags must be attached to withstand environmental conditions.
  • Requirement: If a tag is used (because a lock is not possible), additional safety measures must be taken to provide a level of safety equivalent to a lock (e.g., removing a valve handle).
