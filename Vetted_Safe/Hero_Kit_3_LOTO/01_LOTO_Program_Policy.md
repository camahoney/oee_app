# Lockout / Tagout (LOTO) Written Program
**Compliance:** OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147
**Version:** 2026.1
**Purpose:** To prevent the release of hazardous energy while employees are servicing or maintaining equipment.

---

## 1. Policy Statement
It is the policy of **[Company Name]** to strictly ensure that all machinery is isolated from its energy sources before any employee places any body part into a danger zone.  
**Zero Tolerance:** Failure to follow LOTO procedures allows for immediate termination.

## 2. Roles and Responsibilities

### Authorized Employee (The Person Locking Out)
*   Must be trained on *how* to control energy.
*   Must place their *own* personal lock on the device.
*   Must verify "Zero Energy" before starting work.

### Affected Employee (The Operator)
*   Must be notified when LOTO is starting.
*   Must NOT attempt to restart equipment that is locked out.

## 3. Hardware Requirements
*   **Locks:** Shall be distinct (e.g., Red locks used ONLY for LOTO, not for lockers).
*   **Keying:** One Person, One Lock, One Key. No master keys allowed.
*   **Tags:** Must be durable, weather-resistant, and identify the owner ("DANGER - DO NOT OPERATE").

## 4. The 6-Step LOTO Procedure (Mandatory)
Every LOTO event must follow this sequence:

1.  **Preparation:** Notify affected employees. Identify energy sources (Electric, Pneumatic, Hydraulic, Gravity).
2.  **Shutdown:** Turn off the machine using the normal stopping procedure.
3.  **Isolation:** Physically operate the energy-isolating device (Breaker, Valve). *Push buttons are NOT isolation devices.*
4.  **Application:** Apply the Lock and Tag to the isolation device.
5.  **Stored Energy:** Release or restrain stored energy (Bleed air, block raised rams, discharge capacitors).
6.  **Verification (The Most Important Step):**
    *   Ensure all personnel are clear.
    *   Attempt to start the machine ("Tryout").
    *   **Return controls to Neutral.**

## 5. Shift Change & Group LOTO
*   **Shift Change:** Use a "Transition Lock". The oncoming shift applies their lock *before* the outgoing shift removes theirs.
*   **Group LOTO:** Use a Group Lockbox.
    *   One "Primary" lock on the machine key/hasp.
    *   Key placed in box.
    *   All workers attach personal locks to the box.

## 6. Removal of LOTO (Restoring Service)
1.  Inspect machine components (ensure no tools left inside).
2.  Notify affected employees.
3.  Remove Lock/Tag (Only by the owner).
4.  Energize and Test.

## 7. Emergency Lock Removal
If the lock owner is not on site and the lock must be cut:
1.  Verify the owner is consistently absent (call cell phone).
2.  Get authorization from Plant Manager.
3.  Cut the lock.
4.  **Notify the owner immediately upon their return** that their lock was removed.

## 8. Training Frequency
*   **Authorized:** Annually + Practical Review.
*   **Affected:** Upon hire.
*   **Retraining:** Required if an audit fails or a near-miss occurs.
