01. The Cost of Shortcuts

  • 3 million workers service equipment annually.
  • ~120 fatalities per year from unlawful startup.
  • ~50,000 injuries (amputations, crushing).
Reality Check: It takes 5 minutes to lock out a machine. It takes 0.5 seconds to lose a hand.

03. When is LOTO Required?

LOTO is required anytime you are:

  • Removing or bypassing a guard.
  • Placing any part of your body into the "Point of Operation".
  • Installing, repairing, adjusting, or cleaning equipment.
Exception (Cord & Plug): If you can unplug a fan and keep the plug in your hand, you don't need a lock.

04. Hardware Rules

  • Standardized: We use RED locks for LOTO. Nothing else.
  • Exclusive: Never use a LOTO lock for your gym locker or tool box.
  • Identifiable: Every lock must have a tag or label with YOUR name.
  • Durable: Must withstand the environment (heat/chemical).

05. The "Fatal 6" Steps

  1. Notify: Tell the operator you are shutting it down.
  2. Shutdown: Use normal stop buttons.
  3. Isolate: Open breakers, close valves.
  4. Lock: Apply your lock and tag.
  5. Dissipate: Bleed pressure, block gravity.
  6. VERIFY: Try to start it. (Most important!)

07. Group Lockout

When more than one person works on a machine:

  • Use a Group Lockbox OR a Multi-Lock Hasp.
  • Every single person must have their OWN lock on the machine.
  • Never rely on someone else's lock to protect your life.
"One Person, One Lock, One Key." No exceptions.

08. Shift Changes

The Handoff

2nd Shift applies their lock before 1st Shift removes theirs.

The Gap

If there is a gap, the machine must remain locked. Use a Department Transition Lock if needed.
