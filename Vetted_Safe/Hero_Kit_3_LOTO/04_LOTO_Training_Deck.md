# Lockout / Tagout (LOTO) Training: Control of Hazardous Energy
**Version:** 1.0
**Format:** PowerPoint (15 Slides)
**Target:** Authorized & Affected Employees

---

## Slide 1: The "Fatal Five"
**Visual:** Graphic showing "Struck By, Caught In, Electrocution".
**Text:** Why are we here?
*   LOTO violations are in OSHA's Top 10 every year.
*   **The Reality:** Machines don't know the difference between metal and flesh. If it turns on while you are inside, you will not survive.
**Speaker Notes:** "This isn't about paperwork. It's about ensuring the machine is DEAD before you touch it."

## Slide 2: Definitions
**Visual:** Split screen: Mechanic with Lock vs Operator with Button.
**Text:**
*   **Authorized Employee:** The person who locks it out to service it. (Mechanics, Maintenance).
*   **Affected Employee:** The operator whose machine is shut down.
*   **Rule:** Affected employees must NEVER remove a lock or attempt to restart.

## Slide 3: The Hardware
**Visual:** Photo of Company Standard Red Lock and Tag.
**Text:**
*   **Gold Rule:** One Person, One Lock, One Key.
*   **Never:** Never lend your key. Never rely on someone else's lock.
*   **Tags:** Tags are basically warning signs. They provide NO physical protection.

## Slide 4: The 6 Steps to Life (Steps 1-3)
**Visual:** Icons 1, 2, 3.
**Text:**
1.  **Preparation:** Know what kills you (Volts? PSI? Gravity?).
2.  **Shutdown:** Normal stop button.
3.  **Isolation:** Open the breaker. Close the valve. (Pulling a fuse is not enough).

## Slide 5: The 6 Steps to Life (Steps 4-6)
**Visual:** Icons 4, 5, 6 (Emphasis on #6).
**Text:**
4.  **Application:** Put your lock on.
5.  **Stored Energy:** Bleed the air. Block the ram. Disconnect the capacitor.
6.  **Verification (The Try-Out):** Try to turn it on. If it stays off, you are safe.

## Slide 6: The "Group Lockout" Scenerio
**Visual:** A Lockbox with multiple locks.
**Text:**
*   **Scenario:** 5 people working on one machine.
*   **Solution:** The machine key goes in the box. Everyone puts their lock on the box.
*   **Result:** The key cannot come out until the *last* person removes their lock.

## Slide 7: Shift Change
**Visual:** Hand-off graphic.
**Text:**
*   Never leave a machine unlocked if it's still dangerous.
*   **Hand-off:** Oncoming shift applies lock -> Outgoing shift removes lock.
*   **Gap?** If no one is there, leave your lock on.

## Slide 8: Emergency Removal
**Visual:** Bolt cutters (with a warning sign).
**Text:**
*   If you leave with your key, and we need the machine...
*   We will call you.
*   If we can't reach you, we will cut your lock.
*   **Consequence:** We will have a very serious conversation when you return.

## Slide 9: Common Mistakes (How People Die)
**Visual:** "Caution" tape.
**Text:**
1.  Trusting a "Stop" button/Emergency Stop (These are circuits, not isolation!).
2.  Assuming someone else locked it out.
3.  Forgetting gravity (Hydraulic press drifting down).
4.  Not verifying (Skipping the "Try-Out").

## Slide 10: Examination
**Visual:** Quiz time.
**Text:**
1.  Who can remove your lock? (Only you).
2.  Is a "Stop" button a LOTO device? (No).
3.  What is the last step before working? (Verification).
**Speaker Notes:** "Let's review the answers..."
