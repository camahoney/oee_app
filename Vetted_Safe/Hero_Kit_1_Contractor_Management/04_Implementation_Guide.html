Phase 1: The Strategy

The Hidden Liability

For most companies, the #1 unchecked risk is not their own employees—it's the contractors they hire. Electricians, plumbers, and maintenance techs often work without the same oversight as your own team. If they get hurt on your property, you are liable.

📈
The Goal: To shift from "Trusting" to "Verifying" without creating a bureaucratic nightmare that stops work.

Core Pillars of This System

1. Pre-Qualification

We do not hire companies with bad safety records. The Prequal Form (PQF-01) acts as the first filter.

2. Orientation

No one touches a tool until they know our rules. The Orientation Deck ensures a baseline of knowledge.

3. Insurance Check

The Waiver & COI protects our balance sheet. If they aren't insured, they don't enter.

4. Accountability

We use the Violation Ticket (optional add-on) to track ongoing performance.

Topic: Step-by-Step

Phase 2: The Rollout

Step 1: The "New Rules" Notification

Send an email to your Purchasing Agents and Front Desk Reception. They are the gatekeepers.

"Effective [DATE], no Purchase Order will be issued to a new contractor unless they have a completed 'Green Light' Prequalification Form on file."

Step 2: Building the Approved List

Don't try to boil the ocean. Start with your Top 5 most frequent vendors.

  1. Email them the Prequalification Form (PQF-01).
  2. Review their OSHA Logs (Page 1 of Form). If they have fatalities, flag them for review.
  3. Request their COI. Check the expiration date.
  4. Save approved vendors in a folder called "Green List".

Step 3: The "Day Of" Protocol

When a technician arrives at the front door:

  • Reception: Hands them the "Visitor Rules" (or shows the slides on a tablet).
  • Technician: Reads the slides (5-10 mins).
  • Signature: Technician signs the log book confirming they understood.
  • Result: They are now legally "warned" and trained.

Phase 3: Handling Pushback

Scenario A: "We don't have time for this."

Contractor says: "I've been working here for 20 years. I don't need to read safety slides."

You say: "I know you know your stuff, Bob. But our insurance requires everyone to have a fresh signature on file this year. It'll only take 5 minutes, and then you're good for the whole year."

Scenario B: "Our insurance is private."

Contractor says: "We don't share our EMR or insurance costs."

You say: "We don't need costs. We just need a standard Certificate of Insurance (COI) to verify coverage. This is standard industry practice for any commercial client."

Scenario C: "Emergency Repair"

Situation: A pipe burst at 2 AM. Do we make them fill out a 4-page form?

Protocol: No. Use the Liability Waiver (LEGAL-01). It is a one-page "Emergency Brake" that protects you legally without slowing down the critical repair. Do the full paperwork later.

Success Metric: Aim to have 100% of your "Top 10" vendors pre-qualified within 30 days of launching this kit.
