# Contractor Orientation Deck (Content & Script)
**Version:** 1.0
**Format:** PowerPoint (10 Slides)
**Purpose:** To train contractors on site rules, safety expectations, and emergency procedures BEFORE they start work.

---

## Slide 1: Welcome to [Company Name]
**Visual:** Large Vetted Safe "Split Overlay" style. Site photo + Logo.
**Text:** Contractor Safety Orientation. "Safety Starts with YOU."
**Speaker Notes:** "Welcome to our facility. Our goal is 100% safety compliance. This orientation is mandatory for all contractors and subcontractors. Please sign the attendance sheet now."

## Slide 2: Your Responsibility (Stop Work Authority)
**Visual:** "STOP" icon (Red #F43E5C).
**Text:**
*   You are responsible for your safety and the safety of those around you.
*   **Stop Work Authority:** If you see something unsafe, STOP the job. You will NOT be penalized.
*   Reporting: Report all incidents, near misses, or hazards immediately.
**Speaker Notes:** "We have a Zero Tolerance policy for unsafe behavior. But more importantly, we have a Stop Work Authority policy. If you see a hazard, stop the work. We will back you up."

## Slide 3: Emergency Procedures
**Visual:** Site Map with assembly points highlighted.
**Text:**
*   **Alarms:** Fire (Continuous Tone) vs Weather (Pulsing Tone).
*   **Evacuation:** Exit via nearest safe door. Assemble at [Designated Area].
*   **Medical:** Call [Phone Number] / Channel [Radio Channel]. Do not call 911 directly unless instructed (delays onsite response).
**Speaker Notes:** "Know your exits. In an emergency, don't run to your truck—go to the rally point so we can count you. If you call 911, tell us immediately so we can guide the ambulance in."

## Slide 4: PPE Requirements
**Visual:** Photo of worker correctly wearing Hard Hat, Glasses, Vest, Boots.
**Text:**
*   **Mandatory at All Times:**
    *   Hard Hat (ANSI Z89.1)
    *   Safety Glasses (ANSI Z87.1)
    *   High-Vis Vest/Shirt
    *   Safety Toe Boots
*   **Task Specific:** Gloves, Hearing Protection, Fall Harness.
**Speaker Notes:** "This is a 100% PPE site. No exceptions. 'I forgot it in the truck' is not an excuse. Wear it or leave."

## Slide 5: Lockout / Tagout (LOTO)
**Visual:** Photo of a Red Lock and Tag on a breaker.
**Text:**
*   **Contractor Rule:** You must use your OWN locks and tags.
*   **Notification:** You must notify our Maintenance Dept before locking out any equipment.
*   **Simple Rule:** If it's not your lock, DO NOT TOUCH IT.
**Speaker Notes:** "Our LOTO program protects you. Never cut a lock. If you need to work on energized gear, you need a specialized Hot Work permit."

## Slide 6: Fall Protection
**Visual:** Worker tied off at height.
**Text:**
*   **Trigger Height:** 4 Feet (General Industry) / 6 Feet (Construction).
*   **Requirement:** Full body harness + Lanyard attached to 5,000lb anchor point.
*   **Ladders:** Tie off extension ladders. Maintain 3 points of contact.
**Speaker Notes:** "Gravity doesn't care how experienced you are. If you are above 6 feet, you must be tied off. Misuse of fall protection is grounds for immediate removal."

## Slide 7: Chemical Safety (HazCom)
**Visual:** GHS Pictograms (Flammable, Corrosive, Skull).
**Text:**
*   **bringing Chemicals Onsite:** You must have an SDS (Safety Data Sheet) for every chemical you bring.
*   **Labeling:** All containers must be labeled. No "mystery bottles".
*   **Spills:** Report spills immediately. Do not attempt to clean unless trained.
**Speaker Notes:** "If you bring gas, solvent, or glue, bring the SDS. And don't pour waste down our drains."

## Slide 8: Mobile Equipment & Forklifts
**Visual:** Forklift with pedestrian "Halo" zone.
**Text:**
*   **Operators:** Must carry valid certification card on person.
*   **Pedestrians:** Make eye contact with driver. Stay 10ft back.
*   **Speed Limit:** 5 MPH indoors.
**Speaker Notes:** "Our forklifts are heavy and quiet. Assume they can't see you. If you need to drive our equipment, you must be checked out by our supervisor first."

## Slide 9: Hot Work & Fire Safety
**Visual:** Sparking welder + Fire Extinguisher.
**Text:**
*   **Permit Required:** For any welding, grinding, or open flame.
*   **Fire Watch:** 30-minute fire watch required after work ends.
*   **Housekeeping:** Keep combustible trash away from sparks.
**Speaker Notes:** "Don't start grinding until you have a signed permit. And don't leave the area immediately—monitor for smoldering fires."

## Slide 10: Acknowledgement
**Visual:** Checklist with a pen signature.
**Text:**
*   I have received the orientation.
*   I understand the rules.
*   I agree to work safely.
*   **Questions?**
**Speaker Notes:** "Please sign the roster. By signing, you agree to follow these rules. Work safe, and let's have a good day."
