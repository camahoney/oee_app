# Implementation Guide: The Contractor Gatekeeper System
**Status:** Ready for Deployment

## Welcome
You have purchased the Vetted Safe "Contractor Gatekeeper" System. This kit is designed to close the #1 liability gap for small businesses: uninsured and untrained contractors working on your property.

---

## Step 1: The "New Rules" Email
Send this to your purchasing department and reception desk immediately.

> **Subject:** New Contractor Safety Requirements - Effective Immediately
>
> "Team, starting [Date], all new contractors must complete our Safety Prequalification Form before a Purchase Order can be issued.
>
> Additionally, no contractor personnel may start work without signing the Visitor Log and receiving the Safety Orientation. Please see [Name] for the new forms."

## Step 2: Build Your "Approved List" (Use Form 01)
1.  Send the **01_Prequalification_Form** to your top 5 most used contractors (Electricians, Cleaners, HVAC).
2.  Review their answers using the "Stop Light" guide.
3.  **Crucial:** Check their COI (Certificate of Insurance). If it's expired, they don't work.

## Step 3: Train the Gatekeepers (Use Deck 02)
1.  Print the **02_Contractor_Orientation_Deck** slides (or put them on a tablet at the front desk).
2.  Any time a new technician arrives, have them read the slides (takes 5 minutes).
3.  Have them sign the log.

## Step 4: Protect the Business (Use Form 03)
1.  Have the owner/foreman of the contractor company sign the **03_Liability_Waiver** once per year.
2.  File this with their COI.

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
*   **"Pencil Whipping":** Don't just collect the papers. Actually look at the insurance date.
*   **Letting them "Slide":** If they don't have a Lockout/Tagout program, do not let them touch your electrical panels. You will be liable if they get hurt.
*   **Ignoring Delivery Drivers:** FedEx/UPS don't need this. Only contractors performing *work* (maintenance, install, cleaning).
