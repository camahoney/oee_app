02. Site Access & Logistics

  • Sign In/Out: All contractors must sign the Visitor Log at the reception desk daily.
  • Badges: Visitor badges must be worn on the outer garment above the waist at all times.
  • Parking: Park only in designated "Contractor/Visitor" spots. Reverse parking (backing in) is recommended.
  • Escorts: Do not wander. Stay in your designated work area unless escorted.

05. Lockout / Tagout (LOTO)

⚠
Golden Rule: NEVER assume a machine is de-energized.
  • Service personnel must apply their OWN personal lock and tag.
  • Company locks (Blue) vs. Contractor locks (Red).
  • No "group lockouts" without a lockbox.
  • If you see a lock, do not touch it. Violating LOTO is instant termination.

06. Hot Work & Fire Safety

"Hot Work" includes welding, grinding, cutting, or any open flame/spark-producing activity.

The 35-Foot Rule

All combustibles must be cleared within 35 feet of the work area.

Fire Watch

A designated Fire Watch must remain for 60 minutes after work completes.

Site OrientationVetted Safe © 2026Slide 7

07. Working at Heights

  • Trigger Height: Fall protection is required at 4 feet (General Industry) or 6 feet (Construction).
  • Ladders: Inspect before use. Maintain 3 points of contact. Tie off extension ladders.
  • Scissor Lifts: Chain gate must be closed. Keep feet on the floor (no climbing rails).
Site OrientationVetted Safe © 2026Slide 8

08. Chemicals (HazCom)

You have a Right to Know about hazardous chemicals in your work area.

  • Bringing Chemicals On Site: You must provide an SDS (Safety Data Sheet) to the Safety Manager before bringing any chemical on site.
  • Labeling: No "mystery bottles". All containers must be labeled with contents and hazards.
  • Spills: Report spills immediately. Do not attempt to clean up unknown substances.

09. Environmental Stewardship

Storm Drains

Nothing goes down strict drains but rain. No wash water, no oil, no solvents.

Waste Disposal

Separate trash from recyclables. Hazardous waste (oily rags, aerosol cans) goes in designated Red Drums.

10. Knowledge Check

Q1: When is hearing protection required?

A: Whenever noise levels exceed 85 decibels.

Q2: Who can remove a LOTO lock?

A: ONLY the person who applied it.

Q3: Where is the emergency rally point?

A: North Parking Lot Flag Pole.

Q4: What is the fall protection trigger height?

A: 4 Feet (General Industry).
