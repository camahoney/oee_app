01. The Three Rules

1. Certified Only

You must have a wallet card ON YOUR PERSON to operate any lift.

2. Inspect Daily

No inspection = No driving. If it fails, tag it out.

3. Pedestrians Rule

Pedestrians always have the right of way. Even if they are wrong.

02. Forklift vs. Automobile

Automobile

  • Steers with front wheels.
  • 4-point suspension (Stable).
  • Stops quickly.

Forklift

  • Steers with REAR wheels (Tail swing!).
  • 3-point suspension (Tipsy).
  • Cannot stop quickly (Load will slide off).

03. The Stability Triangle

Center of Gravity (CG) must stay inside the triangle.

What shifts the CG?

  • Raising the load (CG moves up).
  • Turning fast (CG moves out).
  • Stopping fast (CG moves forward).
  • Driving on a slope (CG moves sideways).

04. Load Center & Capacity

The Data Plate: Never operate a truck without reading the Data Plate. It tells you the capacity at a specific load center (usually 24").

05. Daily Inspection

Check it at the start of EVERY shift.

Visual (Off)

  • Tires (Chunks/Cuts)
  • Forks (Cracks/Wear)
  • Chains (Tension)
  • Leaks (Puddles)

Operational (On)

  • Horn / backup alarm
  • Lights
  • Brakes (Service & Parking)
  • Hydraulics (Lift/Tilt smooth?)

06. Traveling Rules

  • Keep Forks Low: 4-6 inches off the ground. Never travel with a raised load.
  • Look Back: Look in the direction of travel. If blocking view, drive backward.
  • Speed: Walking speed indoors.
  • Stop Signs: Full stop and horn at all intersections/blind spots.

07. Ramps & inclines

Golden Rule: The Load always points UP the ramp.

Traveling Loaded

Drive FORWARD up the ramp.
Drive REVERSE down the ramp.

Traveling Empty

Drive REVERSE up the ramp.
Drive FORWARD down the ramp.
(Keeps heavy counterweight uphill)

08. Pedestrian Safety

⚠
The #1 cause of forklift fatalities is propertry striking a pedestrian.
  • Eye Contact: Make eye contact before moving near someone.
  • 3-Foot Halo: Keep 3 feet of distance. If they get closer, STOP.
  • No Rides: Never let anyone ride on the forks or the back.

09. Loading Docks

Docks are dangerous. Trailers can move.

Chocks

Wheels must be chocked.

Locks

Dock Lock engaged (Green Light).

Jacks

Trailer stands used if uncoupled.

10. Propane Refueling

  • PPE: Wear gloves and safety glasses (Liquid propane causes frostbite instanty).
  • Procedure: Close valve. Run engine until it dies. Turn off ignition. Disconnect hose.
  • Tank Orientation: Pin must align with hole. Relief valve must point UP.
  • No Smoking: Obviously.

11. Battery Charging

  • Hydrogen Gas: Batteries emit explosive hydrogen. Charge in well-ventilated areas ONLY.
  • Acid: Contains sulfuric acid. Know where the eye wash is.
  • Cables: Don't yank the cables. Grip the connector.
  • Metal: No jewelry. It can arc and cause severe burns.

12. Tip-Over Survival

IF YOU TIP OVER: DO NOT JUMP!
People who jump die (crushed by the overhead guard).
1. Stay in the seat.
2. Hold the wheel tight.
3. Brace feet.
4. Lean AWAY from impact.

13. Knowledge Check

Q1: When do you inspect the forklift?

A: Before EVERY shift.

Q2: You are traveling down a ramp with a load?

A: Backup (Reverse).

Q3: What do you do in a tip-over?

A: Stay in the seat. Brace. Lean away.

Q4: How high should forks be when traveling?

A: 4-6 inches.
