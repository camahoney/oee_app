# Forklift & Powered Industrial Truck Program
**Compliance:** OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178
**Policy:** To ensure the safe operation of all powered industrial trucks (PITs) at [Company Name].

---

## 1. Operator Qualifications
*   **Authorized Only:** Only employees who have successfully completed the training program (Classroom + Practical) and hold a current certificate may operate PITs.
*   **Age:** All operators must be at least 18 years old.
*   **Renewal:** Certification is valid for 3 years.

## 2. Training Program
*   **Training Content:** Shall consist of formal instruction (lecture/video), practical training (demonstration), and evaluation of performance.
*   **Retraining:** Required immediately if:
    *   The operator is involved in an accident or near-miss.
    *   The operator is observed driving unsafely.
    *   The type of truck changes or workplace conditions change significantly.

## 3. Pre-Shift Inspections
*   Forklifts must be examined **before being placed in service** (daily/per shift).
*   Any defects found (e.g., brake failure, leak) generally require the truck to be **taken out of service** until repaired.
*   Records of inspections shall be maintained for [Duration, e.g., 30 days].

## 4. Operating Rules (The "Rules of the Road")
*   **Seatbelts:** Must be worn at all times.
*   **Speed:** Maintain a safe speed (walking pace indoors). Slow down on wet floors.
*   **Forks:** Keep forks low (4-6 inches) while traveling. Never travel with a load elevated.
*   **Passengers:** **NO PASSENGERS ALLOWED.** No riding on forks, no riding on the back.
*   **Pedestrians:** Pedestrians have the right of way. Sound horn at intersections.
*   **Unattended:** A truck is "unattended" if the operator is more than 25ft away. Forks must be down, engine off, brake set.

## 5. Maintenance
*   Only authorized personnel may perform repairs.
*   Modifications: No modifications (e.g., drilling holes in forks) are allowed without manufacturer approval.

## 6. Disciplinary Action
*   Violation of these rules, particularly "Horseplay" or failure to wear a seatbelt, will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.
