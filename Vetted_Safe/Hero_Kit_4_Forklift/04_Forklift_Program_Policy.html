Program Scope
Section: 1.0 General

1.1 Policy Statement

The operation of Powered Industrial Trucks (forklifts) carries inherent risks. It is the policy of the Company to ensure that only trained, evaluated, and authorized personnel operate these vehicles.

1.2 Certification Requirements

No employee may operate a forklift—even for "just a minute"—unless they have completed:

  1. Formal Instruction: Classroom/Online training (e.g., The "Forklift Commander" Deck).
  2. Practical Training: Hands-on demonstration by a trainer.
  3. Evaluation: A documented performance test (Skills Evaluation Form).
Expiration: Certification is valid for 3 years. Operators must be re-evaluated every 3 years or immediately after an accident/unsafe act.

1.3 Operator Responsibilities

  • Inspect the vehicle before use.
  • Report all accidents/near misses immediately.
  • Never defeat safety guards or interlocks.
  • Yield to pedestrians at all times.

Operating Rules
Section: 2.0 Operations

2.1 Traveling

  • Forks: Keep forks 4-6 inches off the floor. Never travel with raised forks.
  • Visibility: Look in direction of travel. If load blocks view, drive in reverse.
  • Speed: Do not exceed walking speed.
  • Seat Belt: Must be worn at all times. No exceptions.

2.2 Pedestrian Safety

Operators must assume pedestrians do NOT see them.
  • Sound horn at all intersections and blind spots.
  • Maintain a 3-foot "Safe Zone" from all pedestrians.
  • Never allow anyone to stand or pass under the elevated portion of the truck.

2.3 Loading Docks

Trailers are dangerous. Before entering:

  • Verify trailer wheels are chocked.
  • Engage Dock Lock (if equipped).
  • Inspect the trailer floor for weakness/holes step-by-step.

Maintenance
Section: 3.0 Service

3.1 Inspection Protocol

Forklifts must be inspected Daily (or before each shift in 24/7 operations). Any defect found that affects safety requires the truck to be taken out of service.

Tagout: Place a "DO NOT OPERATE" tag on the key/steering wheel. Give the key to the supervisor.

3.2 Refueling (Liquid Propane)

  • Shut off engine.
  • Wear gloves and eye protection (propane causes freeze burns).
  • Replace tank making sure the relief valve points UP.
  • Check for leaks (listen/smell).

3.3 Recharging (Electric)

  • Park in designated Charging Area.
  • Apply parking brake.
  • Do not smoke or use open flames (Hydrogen gas risk).
  • Keep vent caps clear.

Enforcement
Section: 4.0 Discipline

4.1 Disciplinary Policy

Unsafe operation of a forklift is a serious offense. The following strike system applies:

1st Offense

Written Warning + Immediate Retraining/Recertification.

2nd Offense

3-Day Suspension + Revocation of license for 30 days.

3rd Offense

Termination of employment.

Zero Tolerance Items

The following will result in immediate termination (skipping strikes):

  • Traveling with a person on the forks.
  • Horseplay / Racing.
  • Operating under the influence of drugs/alcohol.
