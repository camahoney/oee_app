# Forklift Operator Safety Training (Classroom)
**Compliance:** OSHA 29 CFR 1910.178
**Version:** 1.0
**Format:** PowerPoint (20 Slides)
**Purpose:** To provide the formal instruction required BEFORE an operator touches a forklift.

---

## Slide 1: Welcome to the Cockpit
**Visual:** High-impact shot of a forklift in a warehouse.
**Text:** Professional Forklift Operator Training.
**Speaker Notes:** "Driving a forklift is not like driving a car. It steers from the rear, it's 3x heavier, and it has no brakes on the front wheels. Today we learn how to handle this machine without killing anyone."

## Slide 2: The Statistics
**Visual:** Graph of accident types (Tip-overs, Struck-by).
**Text:**
*   **#1 Killer:** Tip-overs (Driver jumps out and gets crushed).
*   **Golden Rule:** If it tips, DO NOT JUMP.
    *   Stay in the seat.
    *   Hold the wheel.
    *   Brace your feet.
    *   Lean away from the impact.

## Slide 3: The Stability Triangle (Physics)
**Visual:** Diagram of a forklift showing the Center of Gravity (CG) shifting.
**Text:**
*   **The Triangle:** The front two wheels and the center of the rear axle form a triangle.
*   **The Rule:** Keep the Center of Gravity inside the triangle.
*   **What shifts it?**
    *   Lifting high (moves CG up).
    *   Turning fast (moves CG side).
    *   Fast stopping (moves CG forward).

## Slide 4: Pre-Shift Inspections
**Visual:** Photo of an operator checking the tires/hoses.
**Text:**
*   **Mandatory:** You must inspect the lift *before* every shift.
*   **Checklist:** Leaks, Brakes, Horn, Steering, Forks (cracks/bends).
*   **If it fails:** Remove the key. Tag it "Out of Service". Tell your supervisor.

## Slide 5: Load Handling Basics
**Visual:** Diagram of carrying a load low vs high.
**Text:**
*   **Carry Low:** Keep forks 4–6 inches off the ground.
*   **Tilt Back:** Stabilize the load against the backrest.
*   **Visibility:** If the load blocks your view, drive backwards (except on ramps).

## Slide 6: Pedestrians (The danger zone)
**Visual:** Icon of a person walking near a forklift with a "No" sign.
**Text:**
*   **Right of Way:** Pedestrians ALWAYS have the right of way.
*   **Eye Contact:** Do not move until you lock eyes with the walker.
*   **Blind Spots:** You have huge blind spots. Assume people are walking into them.
*   **Horns:** Use at every intersection and blind corner.

## Slide 7: Ramps and Inclines
**Visual:** Icon of forklift on slope.
**Text:**
*   **Loaded:** Forks upgrade (Keep the load from sliding off).
*   **Unloaded:** Forks downgrade (Keep the heavy counterweight uphill).
*   **Never:** Never turn on a ramp. You will tip over.

## Slide 8: Refueling / Recharging
**Visual:** Battery charging station / Propane tank change.
**Text:**
*   **Propane:** Wear gloves (Liquid propane freezes skin instantly). Shut valve off while engine runs to clear lines.
*   **Battery:** Watch for acid spills. Hydrogen gas is explosive—no smoking in charging areas.

## Slide 9: Parking
**Visual:** Properly parked lift.
**Text:**
*   Forks Flat on Floor.
*   Controls in Neutral.
*   Parking Brake Set.
*   Engine Off.

## Slide 10: Conclusion & Exam
**Visual:** Text "You are responsible for this machine."
**Text:**
*   Next Step: The Written Exam (Must score 80%).
*   Final Step: The Practical Evaluation (Driving Test).
*   **Certification Validity:** 3 Years (unless you crash or engage in unsafe driving).
