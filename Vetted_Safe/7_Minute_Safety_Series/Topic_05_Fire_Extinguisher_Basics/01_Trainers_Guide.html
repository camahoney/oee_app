PASS Technique

1. Value Proposition & Goals

Goal: Stop the panic. Teach employees the clear "Fight or Flee" logic.

The Stat: 90% of fires can be extinguished if caught early. But hesitation causes total loss.

Preparation Checklist
  • [ ] Projector for Slide Deck.
  • [ ] Prop: Training Extinguisher (Empty) or Mock-up.
  • [ ] Site Map showing Extinguisher Locations.
2. Classes of Fire

Not all fires are the same. Using water on grease spreads the disaster.

  • Class A: Ash (Wood, Paper, Trash).
  • Class B: Barrel (Gas, Oil, Liquids).
  • Class C: Current (Electrical).
  • Class K: Kitchen (Grease/Fat).

Trainer Emphasis

"Evacuation is never 'wrong'. If you are scared, GET OUT. You are not a firefighter."

Module 1: Decide
3. Fight or Flee? (The Decision Matrix)

We use a Traffic Light system. Show the visual aids.

GREEN LIGHT (GO)
  • Fire is small (Trash can size).
  • Air is safe (No thick smoke).
  • You have an Escape Route behind you.
RED LIGHT (STOP & EVACUATE)
  • Fire is spreading fast.
  • Smoke is filling the room.
  • You are trapped or unsure.

Module 2: Action
4. The PASS Technique

Drill this acronym until it is muscle memory.

  1. P - Pull: Break the plastic seal. Pin comes out.
  2. A - Aim: Low! Aim at the BASE of the fire, not the flames.
  3. S - Squeeze: Handles together.
  4. S - Sweep: Side to side motion.
Micro-Drill:

Have every student stand up and mime the motions. "Pull, Aim, Squeeze, Sweep". Verbalize it loud.

5. Scenarios (Fight or Flee?)

Scenario A: Office Trash Can. No smoke. Exit behind you.

Verdict: FIGHT (Green Light).


Scenario B: Solvent drum explosion. Smoke filling warehouse ceiling.

Verdict: FLEE (Red Light). Pull Alarm.

Competency Check

Q1: What does the "A" in PASS stand for?

A: Aim (at the base).

Q2: You enter a room and see thick black smoke. What do you do?

A: Close the door, Activate Alarm, Evacuate. (Red Light).

Q3: Where do you aim the nozzle?

A: At the base of the fire (the fuel).
