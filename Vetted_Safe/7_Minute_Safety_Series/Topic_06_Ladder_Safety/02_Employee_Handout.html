Ladders
Fall Prevention
⚠
Fact: A 6ft fall is enough to cause fatal head trauma. Respect the height.

5 Fatal Mistakes

  1. Wrong Type (Overloaded).
  2. Wrong Angle (Base slides out).
  3. Top Step Use (Unstable).
  4. Overreaching (Tipping sideways).
  5. Skipping Inspection (Broken feet).

The Duty Rating Code

Look at the sticker on the side rail.

  • Type IA: 300 lbs (Best)
  • Type I: 250 lbs (Standard)
  • Type II: 225 lbs (Light)

Setup

The 4:1 Rule (For Extension Ladders)

For every 4 feet of height, move the base 1 foot out.

Too Steep?

The ladder will tip backwards, throwing you off.

Too Flat?

The feet will slide out from under you.

Inspection

Inspection Checklist

Do this EVERY time you grab a ladder.

Feet: Rubber pads are present and grippy.
Rungs: Clean? No grease/oil/mud.
Latches: "Dogs" locked in place securely.
Labels: Rating sticker is legible.

Safe Use Promise

I will maintain 3 points of contact.

I will keep my belt buckle between the rails.
