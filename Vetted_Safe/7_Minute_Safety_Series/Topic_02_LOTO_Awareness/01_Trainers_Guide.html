LOTO
Energy Control

1. Value Proposition & Goals

Goal: Awareness. To ensure "Affected" employees know when to stop and call for a Lockout, and never to touch a tag.

The Promise: "After this, you'll know exactly how to protect your fingers and life from unexpected startup."

Preparation Checklist
  • [ ] Projector for Slide Deck.
  • [ ] Prop: Real Padlock, Hasp, and Tag.
  • [ ] Prop: Group Lock Box (if used on site).
  • [ ] Printed Handouts for all attendees.
2. The "Why" (Hazardous Energy)

Most people think "Electrical". But we must control ALL energy:

  • Electrical: Zap/Shock.
  • Mechanical: Crushing/Pinching (Gears, Belts).
  • Hydraulic/Pneumatic: High pressure fluid/air.
  • Gravity: Things falling.
  • Thermal: Steam/Heat.

Trainer Emphasis

"Stored Energy is the silent killer. Just because the power switch is off, doesn't mean the machine is safe. Air pressure? Gravity? Heat?"

Module 1: Roles
3. Authorized vs Affected

We must draw a clear line. Crossing this line leads to termination or injury.

AUTHORIZED
  • Has a personal Lock & Key.
  • Performs Service/Repair.
  • Verifies "Zero Energy".
AFFECTED (You)
  • Operates the machine.
  • NEVER applies locks.
  • NEVER removes locks.
  • Waits until authorized person says "Clear".
4. When is LOTO Required?

Whenever an employee places any part of their body into a danger zone during service.

  • Removing a guard.
  • Clearing a jam (The #1 cause of amputation).
  • Lubricating internal chains.

5. Social Engineering "Tests"

Discuss these real-world pressures.

Scenario A: The Jammed Conveyor

Context: A box gets stuck. The line stops. Supervisor is yelling "Let's go!". Conveyor is live.

Discussion: "Do you reach in? Do you grab a stick? Or do you stop and lock?"

Correct Answer: STOP. Notify maintenance or Authorized person. Reaching in = High risk of crushing.

Scenario B: The "Forgotten" Lock

Context: Maintenance finished but left their lock on the switch. They went home.

Discussion: "Do you cut the lock? Do you pick the lock?"

Correct Answer: NEVER verify or remove another person's lock. Call site leadership to follow the "Abandoned Lock Protocol".

6. The 3 Rules for Affected Employees
  1. Do not start equipment with a Tag/Lock on it.
  2. Never remove or bypass a guard.
  3. If you are unsure, ask. Assumptions kill.

Competency Check

Q1: True or False: "Affected" employees can remove a lock if they know the repair is done.

A: FALSE. Never remove another person's lock.

Q2: Name 3 types of hazardous energy besides electrical.

A: Hydraulic, Pneumatic, Thermal, Gravity, Mechanical (Springs).

Q3: What do you do if a machine jams?

A: Stop. Do not reach in. Call Authorized personnel.
