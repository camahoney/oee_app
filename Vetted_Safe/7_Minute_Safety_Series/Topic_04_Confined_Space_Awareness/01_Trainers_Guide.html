Confined Space
Awareness

1. Value Proposition & Goals

Goal: Recognize. Stop. Live. Empower "Stop Work Authority" for unauthorized entries.

The Stat: 81% of confined space fatalities are the would-be rescuers. Trying to help kills you.

Preparation Checklist
  • [ ] Projector for Slide Deck.

  • [ ] Printed Handouts.
2. The 6 Deadly Hazards

It's not just "bad air".

  1. Oxygen: <19.5% causes immediate collapse.
  2. Toxic: H2S (Rotten eggs) or CO (Silent killer).
  3. Explosive: LEL > 10%. One spark = boom.
  4. Temperature: Steam or extreme heat.
  5. Engulfment: Grain, sand, liquid (Drowning).
  6. Noise: Cannot hear the attendant.

Trainer Emphasis

"If you drop your phone in a tank, LEAVE IT. It is not worth your life. Never enter without a permit."

3. The Testing Sequence

Order matters. A wrong order gives false safety.

1. OXYGEN (19.5% - 23.5%)

Must ensure the meter works. If O2 is low, LEL sensors might not work.

2. FLAMMABLE (LEL < 10%)

Is the air explosive?

3. TOXIC (H2S, CO)

Are poisons present?

4. Team Roles
  • Entrant: Authorized to go in.
  • Attendant: Watches from OUTSIDE. Never enters. Summons rescue.
  • Entry Supervisor: Signs the permit.

Module 2: Scenarios
5. Decision Making Drills
Scenario A: The "Quick Check"

Context: Maintenance supervisor cuts the lock to "just peek" at the tank level.

Action: STOP WORK AUTHORITY. Breaking the plane of the opening counts as entry. Air could be deadly.

Scenario B: The Grain Bridge

Context: Grain is stuck. Two workers go in to poke it with poles.

Action: STOP. Engulfment hazard. If the bridge breaks, they are buried instantly. Needs harness + retrieval line.

Scenario C: The Sump Pump

Context: Pump fails in a 6ft pit. Worker jumps in to grab it.

Action: STOP. Heavier-than-air gases (H2S) settle in pits. He could pass out in seconds.

Competency Check

Q1: What is the first gas you test for?

A: Oxygen (O2).

Q2: Can the Attendant enter to rescue a buddy?

A: NO. NEVER. They must call professional rescue. If they enter, they become victim #2.

Q3: When can you use Stop Work Authority?

A: Anytime you see a safety violation (No permit, no test, no attendant).
