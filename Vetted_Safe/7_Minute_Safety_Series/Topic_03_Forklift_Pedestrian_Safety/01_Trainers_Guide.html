Forklift
Pedestrian Safety

1. Value Proposition & Goals

Goal: "People > Production". Zero collisions. Shared responsibility.

The Reality: A 10,000lb forklift vs a 180lb human is "No Contest". The human always loses.

Preparation Checklist
  • [ ] Projector for Slide Deck.
  • [ ] Site Map showing "High Traffic" intersections.
  • [ ] Printed Handouts.
2. Shared Duty Model

This is not just for drivers. It takes two to be safe.

  • Operators: Slow down, use horns, keep forks low.
  • Pedestrians: Use paths, stop at corners, make eye contact.

Trainer Emphasis

"We often blame the driver. But did the pedestrian walk out while looking at their phone? We own our own safety."

3. The Triangle of Danger (Blind Spots)

Show the "Blind Spot" slide.

  • Rear: Massive blind spot (counterweight).
  • Mast: Front view blocked by loads.
  • Pillars: Cab frame blocks view.
The 5 Golden Rules for Pedestrians
  1. Marked Paths: Stay in the green zones.
  2. Stop: Treat aisle ends like stop signs.
  3. Eye Contact: Make the driver acknowledge you.
  4. No Blind Spots: Never walk behind a reversing truck.
  5. Focus: No phones. No earbuds.

4. Decision Making Drills
Scenario A: Crossing the Superhighway

Context: You need to cross the main main aisle. 3 Forklifts are zooming by.

Action: Stop. Wait for a gap. Make eye contact with nearest driver. Do not "Dash".

Scenario B: The Active Dock

Context: You need to check a label on a pallet in the shipping dock. Truck is loading.

Action: Wait until the truck leaves or signals the driver to stop completely. Never enter an active trailer/dock zone.

5. Reporting

Report all "Near Misses". If you almost got hit, report it. That is a free lesson. Next time might be a hit.

Competency Check

Q1: What is the most dangerous spot near a forklift?

A: Behind it (Reversing blind spot).

Q2: Does eye contact mean the driver sees you?

A: No. Eye contact means you LOOK at them. You need a Nod, Wave, or Thumbs up back.

Q3: Can you walk through a forklift isle while texting?

A: No. Distracted walking is prohibited.
