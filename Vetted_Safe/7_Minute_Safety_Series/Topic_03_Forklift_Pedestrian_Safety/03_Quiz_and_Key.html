Trainer Answer Key
RESTRICTED ACCESS
Do not distribute to class.
Remediation: If employee scores <80% (fewer than 8 correct), review the missed questions immediately.

1. B) 3 Feet.
Rationale: The "Halo" rule ensures you are out of crushing range if the driver makes a mistake.

2. B) The back end.
Rationale: Rear-wheel steering causes the rear to swing wide, which is counter-intuitive to car drivers.

3. False.
Rationale: Blind spots block high-viz vests. Only eye contact confirms you are seen.

4. B) Stop, Look, Eye Contact.
Rationale: Like crossing the street, but with heavier traffic.

5. B) Driving in Reverse.
Rationale: If the view is blocked, they must drive backwards. Watch for them!

6. B) No. Never.
Rationale: Falls from forks are a leading cause of fatalities.

7. False.
Rationale: A forklift can veer into a walkway. You need to be alert at all times.

8. B) Crushed between forklift and rack.
Rationale: This is a very common accident during tight turns.

9. B) Underneath the forks.
Rationale: Hydraulics can fail instantly. Never trust the lift mechanism.

10. False.
Rationale: A forklift is solid steel/iron counterweight. It weighs ~9,000 lbs vs a 3,000 lb car.
