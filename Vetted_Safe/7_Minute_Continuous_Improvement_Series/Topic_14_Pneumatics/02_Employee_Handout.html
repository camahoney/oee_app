Golden Rule: Air is Energy. Treat a pressurized hose like a loaded gun.

1. The Whip Hazard

Figure 1: Uncontrolled Hose.

Prevention

If a hose disconnects, it will thrash violently.

  • Inspect Couplings: Check for worn "ears" on quick-connects.
  • Install Whip Checks: Safety cables that bridge the connection.
  • Don't Drag: Dragging hoses by the tool weakens the crimp.
MEDICAL ALERT (Embolism):
Never blow air on your skin. High velocity air can enter the bloodstream through tiny cuts/pores, causing heart attack or stroke.

2. Know Your FRL

Figure 2: Filter, Regulator, Lubricator.

F - Filter

Removes water. Task: Check the bowl daily. Drain water if visible.

R - Regulator

Controls PSI. Task: Verify it matches the machine spec (usually 80-90 PSI).

L - Lubricator

Oils the tool. Task: Refill oil when low. Use correct oil type.

3. Pre-Use Checklist

Hoses: No cuts, bubbles, or abrasions.
Couplings: Lock securely. Whip checks installed?
Leaks: Listen for hiss. Tag if found.
Nozzle: Must be "Safety Tip" (vents side pressure if blocked).

4. LOTO Procedure

To Safely Depressurize:

  1. Turn off Main Air Supply Valve.
  2. Apply Lock and Tag.
  3. Open Bleed Valve (if equipped) OR trigger the tool until empty.
  4. Verify gauge reads "0".
