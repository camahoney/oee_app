Pneumatics Safety

Compressed Air & Stored Energy

Vetted Safe | Continuous Improvement Series

Learning Objective

"To treat compressed air as a dangerous energy source, not a cleaning tool. Pneumatics can kill."

The Whip Hazard

If a coupling fails, the hose becomes a whip. It moves fast enough to break bones.

Solution: Always use a Whip Check cable.

Don't Dust Off!

Compressed air can penetrate skin and inject bubbles into your blood. This causes a stroke or heart attack.

The FRL Unit

  • Filter: Removes water (drain it daily).
  • Regulator: Sets the PSI (don't max it out).
  • Lubricator: Oils the tool (keep it full).

Stored Energy

Disconnecting the air supply does NOT remove the danger.

  • Air is compressible (like a spring).
  • Trapped air in a cylinder can launch a piston.
  • LOTO: You must "Bleed Off" the residual pressure.

The Cost of Leaks

  • Air is one of the most expensive utilities.
  • A 1/4" leak = $8,000/year.
  • Action: If you hear a hiss, Tag It.

Final Review

  • 1. Never use air to clean your body.
  • 2. Use Whip Checks on all hoses.
  • 3. Bleed pressure before working.
  • 4. Drain water traps daily.
