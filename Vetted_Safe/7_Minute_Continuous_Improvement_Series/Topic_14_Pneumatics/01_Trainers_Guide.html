Pneumatics
Compressed Air Safety

Session Overview

Duration: 30-45 Minutes

Goal: To shift the mindset of compressed air from "harmless wind" to "stored hazardous energy". Trainees will learn proper handling, components (FRL), and isolation procedures.

Preparation Checklist
  • [ ] Projector/TV.
  • [ ] Prop: Air Nozzle (Safety Tip style vs Illegal open tip).
  • [ ] Prop: Whip Check cable.
  • [ ] Sample: Damaged air hose (if available).
Core Concepts

1. Embolism Risk: Air in the bloodstream moves to the heart/brain. Fatal.

2. Whip Hazard: A loose hose is an unpredictable weapon.

3. Stored Energy: Air acts like a compressed spring. It waits to strike.

Instructor Notes

"The most common violation in factories is `cleaning off with air`. It spreads dust into the breathing zone and risks embolism. Be firm on this rule."

Module 1: The Hazards
Slide 3: The Whip Hazard

Scenario: You disconnect a tool, but the line is pressurized. The hose flies out of your hand and whips around.

Physics: The escaping air acts like a jet engine, propelling the hose end randomly. It can knock out teeth or break an eye socket.

The Fix: Show the "Whip Check" cable. It tethers hose-to-hose or hose-to-tool.

Slide 4: Embolism (Don't Dust Off)
CRITICAL RULE:

30 PSI can penetrate a cut on your hand. 12 PSI can blow an eye out of its socket.

Explanation: "When you blow air on your skin, you risk forcing a bubble of air into a vein. It travels to your heart (Air Embolism). It stops your heart like a vapor lock."

Ask: "What is the only safe way to clean dust off your clothes?"

Answer: "A vacuum, a sticky roller, or a brush. Never air."

Module 2: The Components
Slide 5: The FRL Unit

Walkthrough: "Look at the unit on the slide (or machine)."

  • Filter (Left): Catches water/rust. If full of water, the tool rusts. Drain it.
  • Regulator (Middle): Adjusts force. Higher PSI != Better. It just breaks seals.
  • Lubricator (Right): Drips oil. If empty, the air tool seizes up.
Slide 6: LOTO & Stored Energy

Demonstration: "I turn off the air valve. Is it safe?"

No. The air trapped between the valve and the machine is still at 100 PSI. If you undo a bolt, it will explode in your face.

Procedure: Shut Valve -> LOTO -> Open "Bleed Valve" or cycle tool to release air.

Standard Code (OSHA)

OSHA 1910.242(b):

Compressed air for cleaning must be reduced to less than 30 PSI (via nozzle design) and effective chip guarding must be used.

Assessment

Competency Check

Instructor: Verify each trainee can answer these correctly.

Q1: Why do we use Whip Checks safety cables?

A: To stop the hose from thrashing if the coupling breaks.

Q2: Can I use air to blow dust off my hair?

A: NO. Never apply to body.

Q3: After locking the air valve, what must you do?

A: Bleed the residual pressure.
