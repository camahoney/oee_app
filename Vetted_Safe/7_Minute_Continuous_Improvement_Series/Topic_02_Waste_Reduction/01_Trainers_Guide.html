Waste Reduction
The 8 Wastes (DOWNTIME / TIMWOODS)
🗑
Learning Objective: Trainees will be able to recite the 8 types of Waste (Muda) and identify at least one example in their own work area.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The Concept)

Ask: "If you are walking around looking for a tool, are you adding value to the product?"

State: "No. You are working hard, but you are not adding value. That is called Waste (or Muda)."

Purpose: "We want to work smarter, not harder. To do that, we must kill the waste."

Minute 2-3 (The Acronym)

(Refer to Handout). "We remember the 8 Wastes with the acronym D.O.W.N.T.I.M.E."

  • Defects (Scrap/Rework).
  • Overproduction (Making too much/too early). *The worst waste!*
  • Waiting (For parts, people, or machines).
  • Non-Utilized Talent (Not using people's brains).
  • Transportation (Moving parts unnecessarily).
  • Inventory (Storing parts).
  • Motion (Moving people unnecessarily).
  • Extra Processing (Doing more work than required).
Interactive Scenario

The "Forklift Rally":

"I see a forklift driver drive empty to the other side of the plant to get a pallet, then drive back. He does this 50 times a day."

Question: "Which waste is this?"

Answer: "Transportation (moving the thing) and Motion (moving the person). And probably Waiting."

Minute 4-5 (Value Added?)

The 3 Buckets of Work:

1. Value Added (VA): Transforming the product. Customer pays for this. (Welding, Assembling).

2. Non-Value Added (NVA): Pure waste. Stop doing it. (Walking, Searching, Waiting).

3. Necessary NVA: Waste we can't eliminate yet involved in business/compliance. (Payroll, Inspections).

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's identify our biggest waste right now." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."

Trainer Tips

Motion vs Transportation: This confuses people. Transportation is moving the Product (forklift). Motion is moving the Person (reaching, bending, walking).

Talent: Ask them: "How many of you have an idea to fix a problem but nobody asked you?" That is the waste of Talent.
