Waste Reduction
Lean Fundamentals
🗑
The Golden Rule: Waste is anything the customer is not willing to pay for. If it doesn't add value, eliminate it.

The "Deadly" Wastes (DOWNTIME)

D - Defects
Scrap & Rework

Making bad parts. This doubles the cost because you have to make it twice.

O - Overproduction
Too Much, Too Soon

Making more than the customer ordered. This creates inventory and hides problems.

W - Waiting
Idle Time

Watching a machine run. Waiting for parts. Waiting for a signature. Zero value.

T - Transportation
Moving Stuff

Moving parts from A to B does not change the form or fit. It just risks damage.

Value Added?

Check Your Work

✓ Value Added: Welding, Painting, Assembling, Cutting.

✗ Non-Value Added: Walking, Searching, Stacking, Counting, Inspecting (yes, even inspecting!).

Critical Behaviors

ALWAYS (DO)
  • Speak up if you see a better way.
  • Keep your area organized (5S).
  • Stop the line if you make a defect.
NEVER (DON'T)
  • NO "cherry picking" jobs.
  • NO building ahead just to look busy.
  • NO hiding scrap.
