Learning Objective: Trainees will learn the difference between "Managing" (telling) and "Coaching" (asking), and how to give constructive feedback.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "Does a football coach run onto the field and tackle the opponent?"

State: "No. They stand on the sidelines. Their job is to prepare the players to win."

Purpose: "In the factory, you are the player. The Team Lead is the coach. We need to learn how to talk to each other to win the game."

Minute 2-3 (The GROW Model)

Simple Coaching Framework:

  • G (Goal): "What do you want to achieve?" (e.g., 50 parts/hour).
  • R (Reality): "Where are you now?" (e.g., 40 parts/hour).
  • O (Options): "What could you do differently?" (Let them answer!).
  • W (Way Forward): "What will you do? by When?"
Scenario: The Late Worker

Boss Approach: "You're late. Don't do it again!" (Compliance).

Coach Approach: "Hey, I noticed you were late. We missed our start target. What's holding you up? How can we fix it so you can be here at 7:00?" (Commitment).

Minute 4-5 (Receiving Feedback)

Feedback is a Gift: "Most people avoid feedback because it feels bad. But it's the only way to get better."

The Rule: "Don't defend. Just listen. Say 'Thank you', then decide if it's true."

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's practice one coaching question." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."

Ask vs Tell: "If you tell them, it's your idea. If you ask them, it's their idea." People fight for their own ideas.
