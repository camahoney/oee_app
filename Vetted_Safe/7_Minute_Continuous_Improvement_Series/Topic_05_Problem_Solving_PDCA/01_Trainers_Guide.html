Problem Solving
PDCA, 8D, and A3 Thinking
🧠
Learning Objective: Trainees will be able to apply the scientific method (P.D.C.A.) to solve problems permanently, rather than just "band-aiding" symptoms.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "Has anyone ever 'fixed' a machine, only to have it break again the next day?"

State: "That means we fixed the SYMPTOM, not the ROOT CAUSE."

Purpose: "We use structured problem solving (PDCA) to kill problems dead so they never come back."

Minute 2-3 (The Cycle)

P.D.C.A. (Plan-Do-Check-Act):

  • Plan: Define the problem. Find the root cause (5 Whys).
  • Do: Try a fix. (Pilot test).
  • Check: Did it work? Measure the data.
  • Act: Standardize it. Make it the new rule.
Scenario: The Oil Leak

"There is an oil puddle on the floor. I clean it up (Action). Next day, it's back."

Bad Fix: Just cleaning it (Symptom).

PDCA Fix: PLAN (Why is it leaking? Gasket is blown). DO (Replace gasket). CHECK (Is it dry tomorrow?). ACT (Add gasket check to monthly maintenance).

Minute 4-5 (Tools: A3 & 8D)

A3 Report: "A problem solved on a single sheet of paper. It forces you to be concise."

8D (8 Disciplines): "Used for big customer complaints. It's PDCA on steroids. Includes containing the bad parts immediately."

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's try a quick problem solving exercise on a current issue." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."

Trainer Tips

Don't jump to solutions: Humans want to "Act" immediately. Force them to spend 50% of the time in "Plan" (Defining the problem).

Go See: You cannot solve a problem from a meeting room. Go to the machine/spot where it happened.
