Inventory & Kanban
Lean Material Flow (Pull Systems)
📦
Learning Objective: Trainees will understand the dangers of Excess Inventory and how a "Kanban" (Pull) signals production only when needed.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "Is having a warehouse full of parts a good thing or a bad thing?"

State: "It feels 'safe', but it's bad. Inventory ties up cash, hides defects, and takes up space."

Purpose: "We want Just-In-Time (JIT). The right part, at the right time, in the right amount."

Minute 2-3 (Push vs Pull)

Push System (Bad): "Building based on a guess (forecast). If the customer doesn't buy it, we are stuck with it."

Pull System (Good): "Building only when the customer 'pulls' it. Like a Supermarket."

Analogy: The Supermarket

"When you buy milk at the store, the shelf has a gap. The stocker sees the gap and fills it."

The Kanban: "The empty space (or a card) is the signal to restock. They don't just fill the shelf until milk falls on the floor."

Minute 4-5 (How Kanban Works)

1. The Signal: A card, a bin, or an empty square on the floor.

2. The Rule: "No Ticket, No Work." If you don't have a Kanban signal, DO NOT produce parts.

3. The Loop: Customer uses part -> Signal goes back -> Supplier makes part.

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's identify our Kanban signals." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."

Preparation / Props

  • A Kanban Card (or Bin) from the floor

Trainer Tips

Fear of Running Out: People hoard inventory because they don't trust the system. You must prove that the Pull system works.

Visual Management: If you can't assess inventory levels in 5 seconds from 5 feet away, it's not visual enough.
