5 Whys Analysis
Root Cause Problem Solving
❓
Learning Objective: Trainees will learn to drill down past the "Band-Aid" symptoms to find and eliminate the true Root Cause of a problem.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "Why do children learn so fast?"

State: "Because they ask 'Why?' relentlessly. They don't accept the first answer."

Purpose: "We stop asking too soon. We blame 'Human Error' and move on. That is lazy. We need to think like kids to solve big problems."

Minute 2-3 (The Concept)

The Rule: "Ask 'Why' 5 times (or until you hit a process you can fix)."

Common Trap: "Don't stop at 'He wasn't paying attention'. Ask 'Why is the process confusing?'"

The Classic Example: The Memorial

Problem: The Jefferson Memorial is crumbling.

  • 1. Why? "We wash it too much."
  • 2. Why? "Because of all the bird poop."
  • 3. Why? "Birds eat the spiders there."
  • 4. Why? "Spiders eat the gnats attracted to the lights."
  • 5. Why? "Measurements show we turn the lights on 1 hour too early."

Solution: Turn the lights on 1 hour later. Save the gnats, save the stone.

Minute 4-5 (Application)

1. Assemble a Team: "Don't do it alone. You need different perspectives."

2. Verify the Facts: "Don't guess. Go see."

3. Fix the Root: "If you just bought 'Bird Nets' (Symptom), you'd spend millions and fail. Fix the GNAT problem."

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's try a 5-Why on a problem we have here." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."

Trainer Tips

Don't Blame: If the 5th Why is a person's name, you failed. The 5th Why should be a broken process, lack of training, or bad design.
