Performance Management
Motivation & Data-Driven Feedback
📊
Learning Objective: Trainees will understand that performance is measured by Data (KPIs), not Opinions, and that feedback is a tool for growth, not punishment.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "How do you know if you had a 'good day' at work?"

State: "Most people say 'I felt busy'. But being busy isn't the same as winning."

Purpose: "We need a Scoreboard. A clear way to know if we are winning or losing, every single hour."

Minute 2-3 (KPIs & Metrics)

Key Performance Indicators (KPIs):

  • Safety: (Near Misses, Incidents) - Always #1.
  • Quality: (Scrap %, First Pass Yield).
  • Delivery: (Parts per Hour, On-Time Ship).
  • Cost: (Labor Hours, Material Usage).
Interactive Scenario

The "Blind Bowler":

"Imagine bowling with a curtain in front of the pins. You roll the ball, you hear a crash, but you can't see what happened."

Question: "Is that fun? Do you know how to adjust your aim?"

Answer: "No. Working without Feedback is just like blind bowling. Frustrating and ineffective."

Minute 4-5 (Receiving Feedback)

Feedback is Fuel: "When a leader gives you feedback, it's not an attack. It's course correction."

Data vs Opinion: "We focus on the data. 'You produced 10 parts' is a fact. 'You are lazy' is an opinion. We don't do opinions."

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's look at our department Scoreboard." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."

Trainer Tips

Motivation: Money motivates to a point. Mastery, Autonomy, and Purpose motivate more. Show them why their work matters.

The Ratio: Try to give 3 pieces of Positive Reinforcement for every 1 Correction.
