Standard Work
The Visual Workplace (5S)
📝
Learning Objective: Trainees will understand that Standard Work is the "One Best Way" to do a job, and without it, there can be no improvement.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "If all 3 shifts do the job differently, how do we know which way is best?"

State: "We don't. We have chaos. We have 3 different companies in one building."

Purpose: "Standard Work creates a baseline. We find the best way, teach everyone, and then try to improve it."

Minute 2-3 (Visual Workplace)

5S (Visual Order):

  • Sort: Toss the junk.
  • Set in Order: A place for everything. (Shadow boards).
  • Shine: Clean to inspect.
  • Standardize: Make the rules.
  • Sustain: Keep it up.
The Pit Stop Analogy

"Watch a Formula 1 Pit Crew. 20 people change 4 tires in 2 seconds."

Question: "How?"

Answer: "Radical Standard Work. Every motion is choreographed. Every tool is in the exact right spot. No searching. No guessing."

Minute 4-5 (The 'Standard')

What is in a Standard?

1. Takt Time: The pace we must work to.

2. Work Sequence: Step 1, Step 2, Step 3. (Not 1, 3, 2).

3. Standard WIP: The exact amount of inventory needed to work smoothly. No more, no less.

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's check a shadow board right now." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."

Trainer Tips

Not Robots: Standard Work doesn't make people robots. It frees their brain from "guessing" so they can focus on Quality and Safety.

Living Documents: If a standard hasn't changed in a year, you probably aren't improving.
