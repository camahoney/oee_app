What is Value Stream Mapping?

Value stream mapping is a way to draw how work actually moves from customer request through every step until delivery. It includes both material flow (the product) and information flow (the orders).

It helps teams see where time, effort, and money are wasted so improvements focus on the real problems, not symptoms.

Figure 1: Visualizing the end-to-end flow.

Why it matters to you

  • Fewer Fires: A better value stream means fewer emergencies and smoother schedules.
  • Predictable Days: Less rework and consistent expectations.
  • Safety: Improvements can reduce overtime, cut unnecessary walking, and clear congestion.

How we use VSM here

Step 1: The Walk. A cross-functional team walks the actual process path to build the "Current State" map. Ideally done backwards (Ship -> Receive).
Step 2: The Data. We collect real data (cycle times, rework rates) to base decisions on facts, not meaningful guesses.
Step 3: The Plan. The team designs a "Future State" with better flow and creates an action plan.

Your Role During a VSM

Share Reality

Share how work really happens, including workarounds, delays, missing info, and safety concerns. Don't "dress it up."

Validate Data

Help validate the numbers on the map (cycle times, changeovers). You know the process best.

Participate: Try new ways of working from the implementation plan and give honest feedback on what helps or hurts.

What Good Looks Like (After VSM)

  • Shorter Lead Times: Products move faster from order to ship.
  • Less WIP: Fewer piles of inventory sitting between stations.
  • Fewer Hot Jobs: Production priorities are clear and don't change constantly.
  • Safer Work Areas: Organized, clean, and free of chaotic movement.
