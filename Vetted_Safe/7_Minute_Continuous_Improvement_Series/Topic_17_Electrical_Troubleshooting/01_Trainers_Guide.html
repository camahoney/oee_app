Electrical Basics
Troubleshooting for Operators
⚡
Learning Objective: Trainees will learn basic, safe electrical troubleshooting (checking breakers) while knowing exactly when to stop and call an Electrician.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "Can you see electricity?"

State: "No. You can't see it, smell it, or hear it until it bites you. It is the invisible killer."

Purpose: "We only touch 'Operator Accessible' controls. We NEVER open cabinets unless trained."

Minute 2-3 (The 'Tripped' Breaker)

What is a breaker? "It's a safety fuse. If the motor works too hard, it heats up and 'trips' to save the motor."

The Rule: "If it trips, wait 1 minute (let it cool). Reset it ONCE. If it trips again immediately, STOP. Call maintenance."

The Smoke Test

Scenario: "You smell burning plastic."

Action: "E-Stop immediately. Do not try to find the fire. Cut power and report."

Magic Smoke: "Once you let the smoke out of the wires, you can't put it back in."

Minute 4-5 (Water & Electricity)

The Mix: "Water is a conductor. Never touch a panel with wet hands or while standing in a puddle."

Washdown: "Don't spray high-pressure water directly at electrical cabinets. Seals fail."

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's show where the main disconnect handle is." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."

Trainer Tips

One Hand Rule: If you must touch a switch, keep one hand in your pocket. Prevents current from crossing your heart.

Resetting: Turn AWAY from the breaker when flipping it. If it arcs, it blows the door open. Protect your face.
