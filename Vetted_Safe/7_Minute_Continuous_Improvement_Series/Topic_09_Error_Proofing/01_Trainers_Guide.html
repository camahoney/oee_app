Error Proofing
Poka-Yoke (Mistake Proofing)
🛡
Learning Objective: Trainees will understand that "being careful" is not a strategy. We must design the process so mistakes are impossible.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "Can you engage your car in 'Reverse' while driving 60mph on the highway?"

State: "No. The car won't let you. That is Error Proofing (Poka-Yoke)."

Purpose: "Humans make mistakes. It is inevitable. We don't blame the human; we fix the machine to stop the mistake."

Minute 2-3 (The Hierarchy)

Levels of Control:

  • 1. Prevention (Best): Impossible to do it wrong (e.g., USB plug).
  • 2. Detection (Okay): It beeps if you do it wrong (e.g., Seatbelt Alarm).
  • 3. Instructions (Weak): A sign saying "Be Careful". (Fails 100% eventually).
Real World Example

The Lawn Mower:

"To start a mower, you must hold the safety bar. If you let go, it stops."

Why? "To prevent you from putting your hand in the blade while it's running. It takes the 'choice' out of safety."

Minute 4-5 (Creating Poka-Yokes)

1. Contact: Use pins or shapes so a part only fits one way.

2. Count: If the kit has 5 bolts left, you missed a spot.

3. Sequence: The machine won't cycle until the guard is closed.

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's identify a Poka-Yoke we use daily." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."

Trainer Tips

Murphy's Law: Anything that CAN go wrong, WILL go wrong. Design for the "Bad Day", not the "Good Day".

Simple is Best: Poka-Yokes don't need computers. A piece of tape or a wooden block can be a Poka-Yoke.
