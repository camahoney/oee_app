Operation & Troubleshooting
💻
Learning Objective: Trainees will understand the basic "Input-Logic-Output" cycle of a Programmable Logic Controller (PLC) to assist in safe troubleshooting.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "Does the machine 'know' when a part is present?"

State: "Not magic. It has 'eyes' (sensors). The PLC is the Brain that connects the Eyes to the Hands (Motors)."

Purpose: "If you understand the Brain, you can fix the machine without forcing it to crash."

Minute 2-3 (The I-P-O Cycle)

3 Step Process:

  • 1. Inputs (Eyes): Buttons, Switches, Photo-eyes. (State: On/Off).
  • 2. Processing (Brain): The Program/Mac. "If Eye sees part, THEN start Motor."
  • 3. Outputs (Hands): Motors, lights, valves. (Action).
Troubleshooting Scenario

Problem: The conveyor won't start.

Check the Lights: "Look at the Input Module. Is the 'Start Button' light on? No? Then the PLC doesn't know you are pushing it."

Lesson: "Check the I/O lights before you call maintenance."

Minute 4-5 (Safety First)

Forcing Inputs: "Never tape back a sensor or block a photo-eye to 'trick' the PLC."

Why? "The PLC assumes safety checks are met. If you lie to the PLC, it might crush your hand."

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's look at the PLC cabinet (from the outside)." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."

Trainer Tips

Binary: PLCs think in 1s and 0s. It's either ON or OFF. There is no 'Maybe'. Teach operators to think like the machine.

Don't touch code: Operators touch hardware (sensors). Maintenance touches code. Draws a clear line.
