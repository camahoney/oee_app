Machine Operator Basics
⚙
Learning Objective: Trainees will understand the universal rules of operating machinery: Respect the Envelope, Test the E-Stop, and Never Bypass Guards.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "Does the robot have feelings?"

State: "No. It doesn't care if it is welding a car part or your arm. It just knows 'Go to Point A'."

Purpose: "You must respect the machine. It is stronger, faster, and dumber than you."

Minute 2-3 (The Envelope)

The Kill Zone: "The area where the machine moves is called the 'Envelope'. Never enter it while the machine has power."

Guarding: "Guards are there to define the envelope. If you remove a guard, you are erasing the line between 'Safe' and 'Dead'."

The E-Stop Test

The Drill:

"Every shift, the first thing you do is hit the E-Stop."

Why? "To make sure it works. You don't want to find out the E-Stop is broken when your sleeve is caught in the roller."

Minute 4-5 (Focus & Distraction)

The Trance: "Operating a machine can be boring. You zone out. That is when accidents happen."

The Rule: "No phones. No earbuds (unless approved). Eyes on the process."

Jam Clearing: "90% of injuries happen when clearing a jam. STOP. LOTO. CLEAR."

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's test the E-Stop on this unit right now." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."

Trainer Tips

"It was just one part": Most people bylaws guards because "it takes too long to stop". That one part is not worth a finger.

Cycle Time: Don't sacrifice safety for rate. If the machine is unsafe, shut it down.
