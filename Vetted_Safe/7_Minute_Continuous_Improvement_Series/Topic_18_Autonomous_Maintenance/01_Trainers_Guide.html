Step 1: Clean Up
🪞
Learning Objective: Trainees will perform the C.L.I.T. routine (Clean, Lubricate, Inspect, Tighten) to restore their machine to "Like New" condition.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "What happens if you never brush your teeth?"

State: "They decay. The dentist can't fix it if you don't do your daily brushing. AM is 'Brushing Your Teeth' for the machine."

Purpose: "We restore basic conditions so the machine can run at full speed without stopping."

Minute 2-3 (The C.L.I.T. Method)

C - Clean: Remove the grime. Grime hides cracks.

L - Lubricate: If it moves, it needs oil.

I - Inspect: Look for wear, rust, or leaks.

T - Tighten: Vibration loosens bolts. Tighten them back.

Scenario: The Tag

Fuguai (Abnormality) Tag:

"When you find a problem you can't fix (e.g., a broken shaft), write a TAG."

Red Tag: For Maintenance to fix. Blue Tag: For Operator to fix later.

Minute 4-5 (Hard to Reach)

Improve Access: "If it's hard to clean, you won't clean it."

Action: "Modify covers or guards (safely) to make inspection easy. Use clear windows so you can see inside without opening."

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's identify one 'Hard to Reach' area on this machine." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."
