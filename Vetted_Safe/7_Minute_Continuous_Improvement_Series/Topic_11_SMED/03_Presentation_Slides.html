SMED

Single Minute Exchange of Die

Vetted Safe | Continuous Improvement Series

Learning Objective

"To reduce changeover time by separating 'Internal' work (Machine Stopped) from 'External' work (Machine Running)."

The Pit Stop Mentality

In 1950, a pit stop took 60 seconds. Today, it takes 2 seconds. How? Preparation.

Internal vs. External

  • Internal (Stop): Tasks that MUST be done while stopped (Changing the drill bit, swapping the die).
  • External (Go): Tasks that can be done while running (Getting the next die, finding the wrench).
  • Goal: Move everything to External.

The 4 Stages

  1. 1. Observe: Record the current changeover.
  2. 2. Separate: Identify I vs E.
  3. 3. Convert: Change I to E (e.g., use quick clamps instead of bolts).
  4. 4. Streamline: Practice the Pit Stop.
