SMED (Changeover)
Single Minute Exchange of Die
⏱
Learning Objective: Trainees will understand how to reduce machine downtime during setups by using the "Pit Stop" mentality (Internal vs External).

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "How long does it take an F1 Pit Crew to change 4 tires?"

State: "About 2 seconds. How long does it take us to change over our machine? 2 hours?"

Purpose: "Every minute the machine is down, we are losing money. SMED is about Speed and Precision."

Minute 2-3 (Internal vs External)

The Golden Rules:

  • External Time: Work you can do while the machine is STILL RUNNING (Getting tools, pre-heating dies).
  • Internal Time: Work you must do while the machine is STOPPED (Unbolting usually).
  • Goal: Move as much "Internal" to "External" as possible.
Scenario: The Cooking Show

Analogy: "Watch a TV chef. All the ingredients are chopped in bowls BEFORE they start cooking."

External: Chopping veggies. (Do this before).

Internal: Cooking in the pan. (Do this now).

Minute 4-5 (Streamlining)

1. Eliminate Bolts: "Why use a bolt (10 turns) when a clamp (1 click) works?"

2. Parallel Processing: "Two people working at once is faster than one person walking back and forth."

3. Standardization: "Mark the settings. Don't guess where '100 degrees' is. Mark it."

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's look at our last changeover time." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."
