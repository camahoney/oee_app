TPM Basics
Total Productive Maintenance
🛠
Learning Objective: Trainees will shift from "I run it, you fix it" to "I own this machine." Basic care prevents breakdowns.

Trainer's Script

Minute 0-1 (The 'Why')

Ask: "Do you change the oil in your own car, or do you wait for the engine to seize?"

State: "You take care of it. But here, we ignore noises until the machine explodes."

Purpose: "TPM means Operator Care. If you hear a noise, report it. Clean it to inspect it."

Minute 2-3 (The 6 Big Losses)

Machine Killers:

  • Downtime: Breakdowns & Setups.
  • Speed Losses: Idling & Reduced Speed (Running at 50%).
  • Quality Defects: Scrap & Startup Rejects.
Concept: Clean to Inspect

The Act: "Wiping down a machine isn't just cleaning. It's inspecting."

The Find: "While wiping, you find a loose bolt or an oil leak. You catch it before it stops production."

Minute 4-5 (Autonomous Maintenance)

Operators Do: Clean, Lubricate, Tighten bolts, Key Inspections.

Maintenance Does: Major repairs, Overhauls, Electrical work.

Result: Maintenance isn't busy fixing loose bolts, so they can fix the big stuff.

Minute 6 (Site Specifics)

"Let's check the lubrication points on this machine." (Review sidebar).

"We end with a 10-question quiz."
