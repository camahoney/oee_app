Learning Objective

"To respect the extreme force of fluid power and understand the invisible lethality of injection injuries."

Key Takeaway: Never search for leaks with your hands.

The Science: Pascal's Law

Force = Pressure x Area. A small pump can lift a massive load.

Respect the Pressure

  • Car Tire: 32 PSI
  • Pressure Washer: 1,500 PSI
  • Hydraulics: 3,000 - 5,000+ PSI
At 3,000 PSI, fluid moves faster than a bullet.

The Silent Killer: Injection Injury

A pinhole leak acts like a hypodermic needle. It injects toxic oil deep into your flesh.

"It's just a pinprick..."

  • It looks small on the outside.
  • Inside, the tissue is dying immediately.
  • If untreated: Amputation is likely.
  • Action: Go to ER immediately. Tell them "High Pressure Injection".

How to Find a Leak Safely

Use wood or cardboard. Never use your hand.

Stored Energy

  • Hydraulics have "Accumulators" (Batteries for pressure).
  • Even with the pump OFF, the system can crush you.
  • Rule: Bleed the pressure before breaking the line.

Lockout / Tagout

Lock the electrical. Lock the valve. Bleed the fluid.

Final Review

  • 1. Fluid Power > Muscle Power.
  • 2. Injection Injuries are Medical Emergencies.
  • 3. Use Cardboard, not Hands.
  • 4. Respect Stored Energy (Accumulators).
